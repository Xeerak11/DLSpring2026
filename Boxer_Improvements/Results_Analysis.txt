"We evaluated VLM-gated DreamerV3 on Atari Boxing across two independent runs (Kaggle and Colab platforms, n=53 and n=45 episodes respectively) and compared against a vanilla DreamerV3 baseline trimmed to matched compute (~300K steps, n=44 episodes). Both VLM runs achieved higher mean episode scores than the baseline (26.5 and 18.9 vs 17.2), with a pooled mean improvement of +5.8 points (Cohen's d = +0.28). Both runs reached the first positive score nearly 2× faster than baseline (steps 5,960 and 6,280 vs 10,600). Notably, all 12 VLM consultations across both runs occurred in the first ~50K training steps, suggesting that early-training intervention was sufficient to bias the world model toward more productive trajectories that the agent then exploited throughout the remainder of training. The consistency of the directional improvement across two independent seeds and platforms strengthens our interpretation that VLM-guided reward shaping provides a small-to-medium positive effect on sample efficiency in this dense-reward environment."
